Case Study — 02

Making estate law legible. Heritance Wills.

Role
Freelance Marketing / UX Designer
Timeline
2024 — 2025
Client
Heritance Wills International
Focus
Editorial UX · Social · Events

The Brief

Estate planning is dense, anxious, and easy to put off. The job was to make it feel clear enough to act on.

Heritance Wills International advises expatriates in Malaysia on multi-jurisdiction wills and estates — necessary, high-stakes, and full of jargon most readers would rather avoid. My role was to translate that complexity into content people could actually follow.

Across digital articles, educational resources and promotional material, I designed and iterated UX layouts that sharpened readability and information hierarchy, turned legal and technical language into plain, accessible reading, and kept a consistent brand voice from first impression to enquiry.

Exhibit A

The long-form article

A two-column editorial feature for expats weighing whether their overseas will still protects them. The layout does the heavy lifting: a drop-cap entry point, a clear reading path, and the consultation offer held in a coloured panel so it is never lost in the text.

Entry point
A drop-cap and a strong headline give the eye one clear place to begin, lowering the barrier to a daunting subject.
Reading path
Two measured columns and tight paragraph rhythm keep dense legal detail scannable rather than overwhelming.
The ask, in view
Contact details and author credentials sit in a maroon panel — trust and next step anchored to the page, not buried at the end.
Plain language
Statute references (MM2H, EPF, intestacy) are framed in everyday terms without losing legal accuracy.
Exhibit B

The social campaign

A set of single-message posts that lead with a plain question, hold one idea each, and keep the contact line and wordmark constant — so the feed reads as one consistent, trustworthy voice.

Exhibit C

Events & webinars

Promotion for in-person seminars and online webinars, built so the details that decide attendance — what, who, when, where — are scannable in a glance, with a clear path to register.

Design Decisions

§ 01 — Hierarchy
Make the page choose for the reader
Dense copy was restructured into clear levels — headline, sub-point, evidence — so an anxious reader could find the one thing that applied to them without reading everything.
§ 02 — Translation
Legal language, human reading
Technical and statutory detail was rewritten in plain, accessible terms, keeping legal accuracy intact while removing the friction that makes people stop reading.
§ 03 — System
One voice across every format
Repeatable layout patterns for articles, resources and promo meant each new piece looked and behaved like part of a single, coherent content strategy.
§ 04 — Alignment
Design tied to the business
Decisions were made with stakeholders to stay aligned to business objectives, client communication goals and the firm's positioning as multi-jurisdiction estate specialists.
§ 05 — Funnel
A clearer path to enquiry
Partnering with business development, I mapped cleaner pathways from awareness post to article to consultation, smoothing the customer-experience funnel.
§ 06 — Engagement
Clarity that holds attention
Each layout was optimised for visual clarity and engagement — generous spacing, a single focal message, and contact details always within reach.

What changed

Content that meets worried readers where they are — and points them somewhere.

Clearer pathways
A consistent route from social post to article to consultation, designed alongside business development.
A coherent brand
Articles, resources and promo share one visual language and tone, reinforcing the firm's specialist positioning.
Lower barrier
Plain-language layouts make a dense, easily-avoided subject approachable enough to act on.
